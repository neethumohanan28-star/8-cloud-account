## 3 Cloud service providers and their free-tier comparison note:-
a) **Microsoft Azure**:-
      This service is provided by Microsoft that allows individuals and organizations to build, deploy, and manage applications and services over the internet instead of using local servers or personal computers. It offers a wide range of cloud services such as virtual machines, storage, databases, networking, web hosting, artificial intelligence, and security services. It supports multiple programming languages and operating systems, making it suitable for developing web applications, running enterprise systems, storing data, and performing analytics. ie, Microsoft Azure lets users rent computing resources online so they can run software, store data, and scale applications without buying or maintaining physical hardware.
        
 ** What the free tier/trial includes?**
      > $200 in free credits to use on any Azure service upon signup.
      > Free access to many Azure products with monthly usage limits: compute, storage, AI, databases, networking, etc.
      > Some services are always free once you have an account.

     **Duration**
      > Free credits: Valid for 30 days after account creation.
      > Free services: Many services are free for 12 months after signup.
      > Always free products continue indefinitely with usage caps.

    **Key limitations**
      > Requires a credit card for signup and identity verification.
      > Monthly usage limits apply for free services — if you exceed them, charges may begin.
      > Some free services may be limited to specific regions or quotas.

b) **Amazon Web Services (AWS)**:- 
        This is a cloud computing platform provided by Amazon that offers on-demand access to computing resources over the internet. It allows users to use services such as virtual servers, data storage, databases, networking, and application hosting without needing to own physical hardware. It is widely used for building websites, hosting applications, storing and analyzing data, and running large-scale enterprise systems. In short, AWS lets us rent IT infrastructure online and pay only for what we use, making it flexible, scalable, and cost-effective.
        
    ** What the free tier/trial includes?**
       > Up to $200 in AWS credits when starting a new account, usable on many services.
       > Always Free services: Certain services remain free each month within usage caps — like AWS Lambda, DynamoDB, and more.
       > 12-month free tier: Many core services are free for one year under specific limits (e.g., EC2 micro instances, storage, database hours).

    ** Duration**
       > Free credits: Good for up to 6 months (or until credits are used).
       > 12-month Free Tier: Typical free use limits available for 12 months after signup.
       > Always Free offers: Ongoing monthly free limits.

     ** Key limitations**
       > Payment method (credit/debit card) required even for free tier.
       > Services have usage caps (e.g., EC2 750 hours/month, database hours, storage limits).
       > Exceeding usage caps or credits leads to normal billing.
       > Free Tier may not include all regions or all services.

 c) **Google Cloud Platform (GCP)**:- 
       This cloud computing service is provided by Google that delivers computing resources over the internet. GCP offers services such as virtual machines, cloud    storage, databases, networking, machine learning, and data analytics. It is commonly used to host applications, store data, process large datasets, and build scalable cloud-based solutions. In simple, it allows users to use Google’s infrastructure online to build, run, and scale applications without managing physical servers.
        
      **What the free tier/trial includes**
         > $300 free credits to spend on almost any Google Cloud service.
         > Always Free usage: Some GCP products remain free each month within limits 

       ** Duration**
         > Trial credits are valid for 90 days from signup.
         > Always Free services have no formal end date but have monthly usage caps.

       **Key limitations**
         > Must provide a credit card for identity verification.
         > Credits expire after 90 days or once all are spent.
         > Free services often have strict usage caps (e.g., number of hours, data storage limits).
         > Some free services are available only in specific regions.
